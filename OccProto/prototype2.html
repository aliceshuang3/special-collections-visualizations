This chart graphs the distribution of all common jobs among males and females surveyed in the 1847 census. Each dot represents one individual, where the dot's position is based on the individual's occupation and id (assigned based on where they were listed in the original data). Hover over each dot to see the details for that individual. Use the buttons to toggle between male only, female only, and both displays. We wanted to incorporate a chart that helps illustrate the sense that these data depict the lives of many individual people, so this scatter plot helps do so even though graphing according to id is not necessarily the most logical.
